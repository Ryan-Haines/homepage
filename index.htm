Ready to Strike

My professional experience has spanned a breadth of projects, including the development of OpenFlow management tools, modernizing internet properties including The Nature Conservatory, Epson, and The Scholastic Store, and automating the validation of transactional integrity in enterprise database systems. These projects have revealed the beauty of rich platform-specific tools combined with the flexibility of virtual machines. At heart, I'm an Object-Oriented Scala developer who relishes managing projects with a web of dependencies, and I don't mind debugging into the wee hours chasing bad links or documenting libraries and plugins. In my spare time, I ported to Android a trio of web apps for bird, tree, and wildflower identification. Cumulatively, these have been installed through the android market over 34,000 times with no advertising budget, and tree identification is open-source and serves as a fantastic tutorial for the basic structures required to create a full-fledged applicaition: basic views, querying a database, and drawing data from the cloud.

Who I am

When I'm away from the computer you may find me biking around the city or assembling a killer grilled cheese. Goliath dispute ignited my obsession with the hierarchy of computational resources we utilize daily, and nothing gets me as fired up as improving the relationship between always-connected, single user devices and their outbound transaction center.
