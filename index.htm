Ready to grow

I crash landed in the Bay Area in early 2013 and immediately fell in love with the city. In that time, my professional experience has spanned a wide breadth of projects, from developing SDN management tools to modernizing venerable internet properties including The Nature Conservatory, Epson, and The Scholastic Store. These projects showed me the beauty of embracing rich platform-specific tools combined with the temporality of virtual machines. At heart, I'm an Object-Oriented Scala developer who relishes managing projects with a web of dependencies, and I don't mind debugging for an evening or more to chase down pesky missing links. In my spare time, I ported to android a trio of nature apps starting with wildflower identification in order to make the tool available in the deep wilderness. After completing my first market release, I subesquently ported logically similar tools for tree and bird identification. Cumulatively, these have been installed through the android market over 27,000 times, and tree identification was open-sourced after its release to allow more experienced arborealists to improve the clarity of the filtration algorithm.

Who I am

When I'm away from the computer you'll probably find me riding my bike around the city or honing my Magic: The Gathering skills - I'm a longtime devotee of the game and have played competitively for over a decade in draft and legacy circles. I love to play Systems Administrator when it comes to gadgets - the thrill of being able to modify the space invaders graphics on my iPod in grade school immediately got me hooked on the firmware hacking scene, and I closely followed the now-infamous Sony Vs Geohot case. This was the spark that ignited my obsession with the hierarchy of computational resources we interact with daily, and nothing gets me as fired up as improving the relationship between always-connected, single user devices and their outbound transaction center.

What I've been up to

I'm looking to help you blossom your company in whatever capacity is most needed, from maintaining and scaling your existing application to exploring what type of mobile solution is best for you. I have experience with all parts of this process, from inception to production and continued support. My current pet project is redesigning the website for Mineways , one of the best ways to introduce kids (and adults!) to the fascinating world of 3D printing. I work best in smaller teams where teamwork often happens as an n-clique, but decisions are still made on the fly. I'm ready to join your smaller team of driven and focused individuals to rapidly test and deploy the next feature that propels us all into the limelight.
