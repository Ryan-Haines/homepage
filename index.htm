Flexible, Apt, Eager

I moved to the Bay Area for an internship over a year ago and immediately fell in love with the city. Since my arrival, my professional experience has spanned a breadth of responsibilities from developing a configuration tool for Software-Defined Network management to building responsive mobile websites for properties including Nature.org, The American Breast Cancer Society, and The Scholastic Store. These tasks were accomplished using varying amounts of Python, Javascript/jQuery, HTML/SASS, and a smattering of custom tools where the stack demanded it. At heart, I'm an OO Scala/Java developer who loves managing a project with a web of dependencies and have earned many scars under the Eclipse IDE. In my spare time I released an android port of the webapp located at the first google result for "wildflower identification" in order to make the tool available in the deep wilderness. I additionally ported tools for tree and bird identification . Cumulatively these applications have been installed through the android market over 27,000 times, and tree identification was open-sourced shortly after its release.
