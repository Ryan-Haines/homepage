Ready to grow

My professional experience has spanned a breadth of projects, from OpenFlow management tools to mobilizing venerable internet properties which include The Nature Conservatory, Epson, and The Scholastic Store. These projects showed me the beauty of rich platform-specific tools combined with the flexibility and temporality of virtual machines. At heart, I'm an Object-Oriented Scala developer who relishes managing projects with a web of dependencies, and I don't mind debugging for an evening or more to chase down missing links or document a library. In my spare time, I ported to Android a trio of web apps for bird, tree, and wildflower identification. Cumulatively, these have been installed through the android market over 34,000 times with no advertising budget, and tree identification is open-source.

Who I am

When I'm away from the computer you'll probably find me biking around the city sampling macaroons. I love to play Systems Administrator - modifing the sprites on my iPod hooked me on the firmware engineering scene, and I closely followed the now-infamous Sony Vs Geohot court battle. This was one of the sparks that ignited my obsession with the hierarchy of computational resources we interact with daily, and nothing gets me as fired up as improving the relationship between always-connected, single user devices and their outbound transaction center.

Professional Services

I'll help you expand your company in its most needed capacity, from maintenance and scaling your existing application to exploring what type of mobile solutions are most appropriate. I have experience with all parts of this process, from inception to production and continued support. I work best in smaller teams where teamwork often happens as an n-clique, but decisions are still made on the fly. I'm also available for penetration testing contracts if you're in need of someone outside your organization to evaluate your product either pre-launch or in its infancy.
